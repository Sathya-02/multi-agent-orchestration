Pythagoras' Theorem: A Fundamental Concept in Geometry

Pythagoras' theorem is a fundamental concept in geometry that describes the relationship between the lengths of the sides of a right-angled triangle. The theorem states that the square of the length of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the lengths of the other two sides.

Mathematically, this can be expressed as:

a² + b² = c²

where a and b are the lengths of the two sides that form the right angle, and c is the length of the hypotenuse.
